Alchemy bridged spiritual ideas and real chemical discoveries.

Boyle emphasized experiments and defined chemical elements.

Dalton’s atomic model proposed atoms as indivisible particles.

Lewis, Pauling, and Mulliken described how atoms bond together.

Gibbs and Arrhenius explained why reactions occur (or don’t).

New machines like spectrometers revealed molecular structures.

Slide 11: Modern Innovations

Green chemistry, nanotechnology, and AI models lead the frontier.
