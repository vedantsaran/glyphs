Ancient Beginnings (3000 BCE - 300 CE)

Ancient Egyptian wall painting showing metallurgical processes
3000-1000 BCE

Metallurgy and Early Chemistry

Ancient civilizations in Egypt, Mesopotamia, and China developed techniques for extracting and working with metals. They discovered copper smelting, bronze alloys (Cu + Sn), and iron working, laying the foundation for chemical processes.

300-1200 CE

Alchemy Emerges

Greek philosophers like Aristotle proposed the four elements theory (earth, air, fire, water). Islamic alchemists like Jabir ibn Hayyan developed laboratory techniques and discovered acids like sulfuric acid (H‚ÇÇSO‚ÇÑ) and nitric acid (HNO‚ÇÉ).

The Alchemical Period (300-1600 CE)

Alchemy bridged mysticism and early chemistry, seeking to transform base metals into gold and discover the elixir of life.

Jabir ibn Hayyan (721-815)

Father of Arab chemistry. Developed laboratory equipment and techniques, discovered hydrochloric acid (HCl), and laid groundwork for experimental chemistry.

Al-Razi (854-925)

Distinguished between chemistry and alchemy, classified substances into animal, vegetable, and mineral categories. First to use alcohol for medical purposes.

Paracelsus (1493-1541)

Introduced chemical remedies to medicine, promoted the idea that diseases had chemical causes and could be treated with chemical solutions.
